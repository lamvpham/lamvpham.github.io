Teams/Groups Entity Architecture

Defining the entity information and relationship architecture in the Dashboard 3-D printer ecosystem

Context

Role

During my internship at Formlabs, I spent 10 weeks defining the user/team/group information and relationship architecture in the Dashboard entity ecosystem. The responsibilities that my role entailed included working with the Dashboard product manager and designer to understand and architect a more usable platform due to the addition of enterprise scaling with Fleet Control.

The problem

How might we improve the UX architecture of the entities to eliminate the need for admins to scatter their company printer fleets between teams?

Solution

My final designed prototypes have a new information and entity architecture for Dashboard based on market landscape analysis, architecture concept iterations, and high-fidelity designs sign up, onboarding, and company setting flows. This design affected 4.2% of customer accounts (~50 companies), helping simplify their mental models and user flows.

Scope & objectives

The scope of the project was to research different products with entity structures similar to or different from Dashboard, then extrapolate that information into different information architectures suitable for the platform. Levels of access and permissions need to fleshed out due to lack of internal understanding for accounts and admins. The timeframe of the project was 10 weeks, so I had to keep track of my time!

Preliminary Process

Information Architecture Iteration

Based on the analysis, my PM and I conceptualized six different entity structures. Each have their own pros and cons, alongside their respective market landscape comparisons to similar products. This boiled down to four structures (one of which is the current IA), then we scorecard each based on the goals defined.

After analyzing the different IA options and their trade-offs, we chose option 1 and 2 architectures as they aligned best with our goals compared to the current version of Teams and Groups. They were then iterated upon for further clarity of their respective levels of access and attribute-based access controls (ABAC). (It's a lot to visually digest, I know...)

MVP - Final Designs

Validation

Myself, my Dashboard product manager, and the Dashboard designer got together with the Chief of Product to discuss the two architectures and decide on the strongest direction for Dashboard. After discussions with the CPO, the general direction moving forward is to implement option 1. This option only requires two levels of hierarchy, encapsulates all teams to be in one organization instead. (Progress!)

Results

The wireframes were designed into high-fidelity mock-ups of the Sign Up/Onboarding screens and the Company Settings screens. The chosen architecture with option 1 was mocked up here after a decision by the wider design team to move forward with the option that is easiest for users to understand.
