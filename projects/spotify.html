Discover Together

A platform to analyze two people's Spotify music tastes, then curate a playlist made specifically for them.

Context

Overview

Over the course of fall 2020 and onwards, the team and I designed and developed a user-facing website to discover new music with the people close to oneself. We believe that sharing music with others brings people closer, and by combining people's music taste with their friends, they both can discover new music, together.

Update: We have currently reached to over 28 countries over 68 countries! As well, over 700+ playlists over 1400+ playlists have been created so far :) (as of 18/2/21)

Role

Working with a team of six, my responsibilities entailed designing and developing an accessible and aesthetic design that can correspond on a mobile device, as well as desktop. I focused on the task flow between pages, making sure the front-end matched with the design, and creating a unique aesthetic that's visually pleasing and welcoming to new and returning users.

The problem

Music exploration has been aided by Spotify's Discover Weekly playlists and other recommendation playlists, but these are typically listened to alone. There is a lack of experience with discovering music with others, and Spotify did try that with their Listen Alike concept, where you can compare your music taste to famous artists and podcasters. But we wanted to define an experience for the user and whoever that user chooses, and encourage them to find hidden gems socially.

Tools

Figma, Coda, Hotjar, HTML/CSS/JS

Duration

September 2020 - Present

Soft Launch: Dec. 7, 2020

Responsibility

Interaction Design, Prototyping, UI/UX Design, Data Visualization, User Research, Branding

The solution

Discover Together is a website responsive on both mobile and desktop, where it provides users the ability to curate playlists based on two people's music tastes. By creating a collaborative experience, we automate song recommendations for both users to discover, together.

We are currently soft launched so check us out! If you'd like to make a playlist with me to see how this works, click here :)

Introduction

In the beginning

When I joined the Discover Together team, the project was already conceptualized by Arjun and Trustin, where the backend capabilities were functional. The website lacked the visual appeal, which is where they brought on myself and Ann Li to revamp the design.

If you want to understand the initial backend process, read this article written by Trustin!

The page flow

The flow of pages for this originally consisted of a new user going onto the website and the first page is to authorize their Spotify account. After that, it leads to the second page, where it shares the user their Discover Together unique link and their top 50 songs.

For the second user who clicked the linked shared by the first user, it leads to the Spotify authorization page to access their music data. From there, it goes to the Spotify platform with the playlist loaded up.

Ideation needs to begin somewhere

To identify our objectives for Discover Together and be transparent in what we want to achieve, we first had to ask ourselves, "what is it that we're trying to do?"

After that, we needed to translate our goals into business objectives with success metrics.

The main component that we wanted Discover Together to become as we progress was to curate an experience that music lovers can use to discover new music and bond with others during the process. Easier said than done, but the biggest takeaway that I specifically wanted to get out of this was to participate in the growth phase of a product and understand more thoroughly the translation of designs and development into something bigger.

Prototype

Low Fidelity Mockups

By using the lo-fi screens, it helped the team understand what was essential to be put on the two main screens of the platform.

To understand explicitly how our audience might interact with our website, we wanted to ensure that the content on each page was easy to comprehend, going through the flow. With this, it raised some questions that continue to drive the copies on our website currently as well, which helped me as a designer to understand the backend capabilities.

Refreshing the flow

By understand the content on each page, the emphasis was to have thorough accessibility and understanding of our platform. Having the link to share and create a playlist is important to highlight, and to support that concept, the discussion revolved around how we can flesh out the website from both the perspective of the first user and the user that clicks the link sent by the first user.

The biggest takeaway from understanding our content on each page was that the flow from page to page was our attraction, so that it wasn't just copying your link, and then all a sudden, the user loses a reason to continue staying on the website.

Collaborating with the other designer, we wanted to specifically address the playlist portion of the website. From question 5 that I had above, I discovered that we are able to store data for playlists. Combining this with the developer-friendliness of the Spotify API, we are able to extract data to present playlist data for the people creating it.

This led the team to create a playlist page storing all of the user's Discover Together playlists, and having a flow that allows them to see the attributes and content of the playlists when clicking into a specific playlist.

As we went through our first iteration of high-fidelity prototypes, it became apparent that some of the concepts drawn out were not feasible with development. Due to this, these hi-fi wireframes below showcase the initial version prior to cutting back.

Designing the aesthetic

I undertook the creation of a welcoming and friendly UI that was easy for development and kept a high rentention rate with users. To do that, I created three different landing page designs based off of minimalist styles, keeping in mind the frontend capabilities.

(Note that these are not to finalize the UI, but to create a distinctive aesthetic to the Discover Together Identity.)

After designing these options, I discussed with the team the aesthetic direction that they want this platform to go in. The second option was unanimously chosen by the team for its soft ambient lights theme, where it has a welcoming atmosphere for new and returning users.

Keeping in mind the accessibility of the platform, the design has clear text sections and components are easily readable with high contrast between foreground and background.

Branding

Representing the platform

For the platform to have a distinct brand identity, we needed a logo that people can recognize while communicating the platform's aesthetic direction.

The first logo idea is a play of ocean waves and frequency waves, where both are overlapping each other to show 'two music tastes intersecting'. The second idea resembles over-head headphones being shared between two people.

Once we discussed with the team, the second option moreso provided a distinct and creative identity that represented the Discover Together platform and its purpose. It's definitely a good learning experience, getting exposure to brand design and understanding how to market a platform's identity.

After talking to the backend developer about rotating cover art for each playlist that a user creates, it was noted that it was possible and would be a cute detail that can ehance a user's experience! Using the logo and the aesthetic from above, I was able to create cover art that'll rotate for each playlist.

User Feedback

What we learned after soft launching

One week after soft launching to close friends and family, we had analytics as to how many users used it and the whereabouts of them.

After a week of user feedback, we noticed from monitoring the user interactions that the flow of pages was concise and straightforward for most. Lacking on the platform was quick and easy copy that explains what the platform is, which caused some confusion when first landing onto the platform.

The green sticky notes below showcase the major feedback received for Discover Together, and the pink sticky notes highlight the potential ideas to fix the top priorities.

Hard Launch

After fully launching, what's next?

The day of the launch finally came and went. February 14th, 2021; Valentine's day! The day before was when our partner Aphrodite launched their matches to the UW, UCLA, and USC campuses, which marketed Discover Together as an icebreaker method. We also spread the word in multiple school, career, and student communities, and as well launched on Product Hunt.

As of February 18, 2021, we gained over 1500 new users with a collective total of 1436 playlists and have reached 68 countries!

Conclusion / Reflection

Moving forward towards hard launch

Starting Soft Launch, we began reaching out to a variety of dating startups and organizations to partner with them! It's the perfect match; music is an element that deepens the personal engagement levels between people (like it's its own love language). By partnering with dating organizations, the rate of successful matches can increase as people explore their potential match's music taste.

Starting the new year, we officially partnered up with Aphrodite Project as they head towards their next matching launch! Exciting things moving forward, and as we begin our process to hard launch, we will begin partnering up with more companies around the California/Toronto areas. This initiative is to drive more personal connections between people, driven by music.

What I learned

Reflecting upon the time that passed as I worked on Discover Together with the team, it was a great opportunity for me to work closely between development and design as we begin to officially roll out the platform. Gathering feedback and implementing that was easier to do with a small team because it was more immediate to address the people involved for that, and the business aspect of marketing and partnerships showed me the more holistic approach towards understanding the later stages of a product.

As I move forward with my design experience, I helped define the identity of a product, and what the metrics for success would be for us as we grow our audience and user base. By emphasizing accessibility and ease of use for users, it taught me more about growing a product from the start, and how to further expand my skillset through polishing user flows and the micro-interactions involved.
