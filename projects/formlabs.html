Teams/Groups Entity Architecture

Defining the entity information architecture in the Dashboard 3-D printer ecosystem

Context

Role

During my internship at Formlabs, I spent 10 weeks defining the user/team/group entity architecture (shortforming it to EA) in the product, Dashboard. The responsibilities of my role included working with the Dashboard product manager and designer to understand and architect a more usable platform due to a new addition of enterprise scaling (Fleet Control).

The problem

How might we improve the UX architecture of the entities to eliminate the need for admins to scatter their company printer fleets between teams?

Solution

My final prototypes have a new EA for Dashboard based on market landscape analysis, architecture iterations, and high-fidelity sign up, onboarding, and company setting flows.

Scope & objectives

The scope of the project was 10 weeks to research products with EA's similar & different from Dashboard, then extrapolate the insights into an EA suitable for the platform. Levels of access and permissions needed to be fleshed out due to lack of internal understanding for accounts and admins.

Preliminary Process

Competitive analysis

To thoroughly understand the problem space, a competitive analysis was done to evaluate 3D & non-3D related tools and their EA's (the problem is not unique to 3D printing).

Entity Architecture Iteration

Based on the analysis, my PM and I conceptualized 4 different entity architectures (one is the current structure). Each have their own pros and cons, alongside their respective market landscape comparisons - so we scorecard each based on the goals defined.

After analyzing the different EA options and their trade-offs, we chose option 1 and 2 architectures as they aligned best with our goals compared to the current version of Teams and Groups. They were then iterated upon for further clarity of their respective levels of access and attribute-based access controls (ABAC).

MVP - Final Designs

Validation

Myself, my Dashboard PM, and the Dashboard designer got together with the CPO to discuss the 2 architectures and decided that the most feasible to implement is option 1. This option only requires two levels of hierarchy, was easiest for (new & current) users to understand, and encapsulates all teams to be in one organization instead.

Results

The wireframes were converted into high-fidelity mock-ups of the Sign Up/Onboarding screens and the Company Settings screens.

Moving Forward

Next Steps

With the handoff of the designs to the Dashboard designer, the next step is to further refine the details of the workflows, alongside mapping out the financial pricing plan for enterprise scaling due to the architectural changes.

What I learned

The new EA was well-received by the product teams. The prototypes allowed for overall usage and the edge cases to be mapped out without question.

Throughout this project, it taught me higher-level product decisions and how to communicate that more effectively. Moving forward from this internship, I want to keep tackling complex problems that deal with access and permissions to further refine my skills with role-based designs.
