NOTIFICATION PATTERNS

ARCHITECTING THE UX PATTERNS & INTERACTION BEHAVIOURS ON A GLOBAL SCALE FOR A MESSAGE CENTER

Context

The Problem

How might we help reduce the lack of consistency across all CDK platforms/apps and address the product team's needs for a standardized notification center?

Role

I spent summer 2021 as a UX Design intern on the Radial Design Systems (RDS) team. The project I worked on was about the UX architecture of a new notification center, and how the designed prototype reflects the requirements that product teams needed.

Solution

The final prototypes have uniform product architecture patterns for easier user comprehension. The generalized designs provided flexibility to match the requirements needed from various product teams.

Introduction

Past Iteration Concepts

The initial context for the notification center was to add notifications into a CDK product (back in 2020). The project quickly became too complex, resulting in its abandonment due to scope creep and lack of clarity for its direction.

Scope & Objectives

The scope was 8 weeks to research common UX patterns for notification centers, then extrapolate those insights into general interaction patterns for any CDK product. Establishing foundational requirements on global notification patterns for international product team adoption was needed (with possibilities for a message hub to be integrated).

Preliminary Process

Competitive Analysis & Internal User Interviews

By conducting a competitive analysis on other established design systems (such as GitHub Primer & Uber Base), that helped me to understand other approaches to a notification center.

In addition, there is a CDK product, Text Connect, which is a messaging hub for dealerships and their customers. Talking with that design manager, they showed the current components and patterns being used on that product. With this, I could replicate those interaction patterns and information hierarchy onto the notification center containing the messaging hub for global consistency between products.

Mockup Iteration

Lo-fi Mockups

With those requirements, I designed 3 variations of a notification center user flow: the first one using a dialog window for the center, the second being a full page, and the third being a mix between a dialog window and a full page.

Applying Feedback

Upon iterating, these higher fidelity dialog window designs portrayed two options for switching between alerts and messages.

Translating that to the full page notification center, the options were with tabs or a dropdown.

The feedback given was that to warrant a new page, alerts will need to be less frequent for more important/urgent needs that require attention. In turn, notifications to be filtered for higher important in a workflow rather than diluted with info from toasts that user already knows about.

User Flows

After presenting the user flows and prototypes to the RDS team and other designers, the general consensus was to have a dialog window as a quick view for notifications and an option for a full notification center page for more actionable workflows.

Using that information and feedback, I mapped out the general architecture for the dialog window and full page that the user would follow with these designs.

MVP

Results

The interactions for users are limited to viewing notifications/messages, going into a conversation & sending a message, and going to the full page notification center.

Viewing from the full page center, there are deep links when viewing notifications that navigate out towards the page with that content. This interaction allows for users to stay productive and get more context on what the information displayed in the notification is about, making the notifications actionable.

Validation

I did testing protocols to validate the architecture patterns of the designs, done through usability verification from the RDS team. The designs had the accessible standards of WCAG 2.0 and a11y, and also integrated well with the components already developed; meaning less complexity for developer handoff.

Trade-offs

Due to the generalized aspect of the notification center, specific use cases of other CDK products may not be easily integratable. The components from the hi-fi prototypes were designed and fleshed out to be accessible for a variety of situations, but the high-level functionality of the components are limited to the newly established requirements.

Moving Forward

Next Steps

Future iterations can build upon and guide designers on how to tackle notifications. The foundational designs are finalized and other teams can use the notification components in the UI Kit, future plans for Text Connect align similarly with this approach.

What I Learned

The adoption of the UI Kit components and templates of the notification center is rolling out within the CDK design teams. Through this internship, it helped teach me to process interaction patterns and product architecture from a more holistic point-of-view. Understanding the range of edge cases that apply to numerous different products and aggregating it all into generalized patterns helped me explore the evolution of past needs in their context to future requirements.
