Notification Center Architecture Patterns

Architecting the UX patterns and interaction behaviours on a global scale for a message center

Context

Role

With my internship at CDK Global, I spent summer 2021 as a UX Design intern on the Radial Design Systems (RDS) team. The project I worked on for 8 weeks of the internship is about the UX architecture of a new notification center, and how the designed prototype reflects the requirements and functionalities that product teams need.

The problem

How might we help reduce the lack of consistency across all CDK platforms/apps and address the product team's needs for a standardized notification center?

Solution

The final designed prototypes have uniformity with modern product architecture patterns and accessibility for a wider audience of users. The generalized designs provided flexibility to match the requirements needed from the design systems team and product teams.

Introduction

Old Design Concepts

The initial context for the notification center was to add notifications into a CDK product. The project quickly became too complex, resulting in its abandonment due to scope creep and lack of clarity for its direction.

Scope & objectives

The scope was 8 weeks to research common UX patterns for notification centers, then architect UX patterns on a broader level for any CDK product. I would need to establish foundational requirements on global notification patterns for international product team adoption, with possibilities for a message hub to be integrated.

Preliminary Process

Competitive analysis & Internal product team interviews

The initial approach to understanding and laying out the architecture and interactions of a notification center was to do a competitive analysis on other established design systems. Researching the notification aspects of GitHub Primer and Uber Base design systems helped understand other approaches to a notification center.

I had to look back to the initial circumstances for the task two years prior, where the progression of the first notification center was surrounded by a multitude of questions and nonstandardized UX patterns using custom components. The line between notification, messaging, and task list was very blurred:

In addition, there is a CDK product, Text Connect, which is a messaging hub for dealerships and their customers. Chatting with the design manager showcased the current components and patterns that are being used on that product. With this, I could replicate those interaction patterns and information hierarchy onto the notification center containing the messaging hub for global consistency between products.

Getting into the weeds of it

Lo-fi mockups

From the requirements I set based on the competitive analysis and internal interviews, I designed 3 different variations of the notification center: the first one using a dialog window for the center, the second being a full page, and the third being a mix between a dialog window and a full page.

Discussing user flow

After presenting the lo-fi mockups to the RDS team and other designers, the general consensus was to have a dialog window as a quick view for notifications and an option for a full notification center page for more actionable workflows.

Applying the feedback (iterations)

The initial designs for the dialog window were isolated to that space inside without conveying a means to enter the full page center. These higher fidelity dialog window designs portrayed two options for switching between alerts and messages only.

With the segmented control, users can switch between alerts and messages within the dialog window space with only one click. As well, adding a footer to indicate going to the full page would be helpful for consistency and page flow.

Translating that to the full page notification center, the options were with tabs or a dropdown. The dropdown menu was already a previous component designed and developed, so it was easier on the development side.

The feedback given was that to warrant a new page, alerts will need to be less frequent for more important/urgent needs that require attention. In turn, notifications to be filtered for higher important in a workflow rather than diluted with info from toasts that user already knows about.

Using that information and feedback, I mapped out the general architecture for the dialog window and full page that the user would follow with these designs.

Results

The image below is the dialog window for the notifications, and the interactions for users are limited to viewing notifications/messages, going into a conversation and sending a message, and going to the full page notification center.

Viewing from the full page center, there are deep links when viewing notifications that navigate out towards the page with that content. This interaction allows for users to stay productive and get more context on what the information displayed in the notification is about, making the notifications actionable.

Validation

Testing protocols to validate the architecture patterns of the designs were through usability verification from the RDS team. The designs had the accessible standards of WCAG 2.0 and a11y, and also integrated well with the components already developed; meaning less complexity for developer handoff.

Tradeoffs being done with the design

Due to the generalized aspect of the notification center, the specific use cases of other CDK products may not be easily integratable. The components from the hi-fi prototypes were designed and fleshed out to be accessible for a variety of situations, but the high-level functionality of the components are limited to the newly established requirements.

Moving Forward

Next Steps

Future iterations can build upon and guide current designers on how to tackle notifications. The foundational designs are finalized and future designers from other teams can use the notification components in the UI Kit. Future plans for Text Connect align similarly with the approach for this notification center, and will benefit the product’s progress by cutting design/development costs.

Conclusions and what I learned

The adoption of the UI Kit components and templates of the notification center is rolling out within the CDK design teams. Through this internship, it helped teach me to process interaction patterns and product architecture from a more holistic point-of-view. Understanding the range of edge cases that apply to numerous different products and aggregating it all into generalized patterns, it helped me explore the evolution of past needs in their context to future requirements.
