OAUTH INTEGRATION

Designed the Google Assistant integration to be used with IoT device voice activation

Context

The Problem

With competitors already integrating their smart devices into the smart home ecosystem, Level was behind the trend and needed to adjust to user needs.

How might we future-proof the Level Lock to remain adaptable with current advancements in voice tech and security?

Role

As the lead designer, I researched and designed the MVP for smart device ecosystem integration into Level's IoT devices. Collaborating with the CPO and mobile devs, the project took 3 weeks to implement from research to prototype.

Solution

The final prototype takes into account the technical fundamentals required for integration, including OAuth 2.0 initiated by Google, secondary user verification for high-risk actions, and App Flipping for account linkage. The voice-activation feature for the Level Locks will give a more natural and convenient interaction to the product, which will be a staple function for all current and future devices.

With this feature, it will be shipped out to the ~10,000 accounts registered to the Level app, where an estimated 40% use smart home devices for voice controls.

Introduction

The Scope

The current method of unlocking/locking the Level Lock is by opening the app and pressing a button, or using a physical key. Competitors already have functionality for voice activation with their IoT devices, and by not offering this, the Level product is at a competitive disadvantage in the household smart device domain.

The Objective

Make Google Assistant a part of the onboarding experience for new users to know that the functionality is there, and within settings for current users to learn about, for more seamless user interactions.

The Level Connect is a Wi-Fi bridge for communicating between various wireless devices under the same network. This is the focal point for introducing the Google Assistant integration within the Level app.

Preliminary Process

Qualitative Research

I had to understand the technical documentation for OAuth 2.0: an authorization protocol to grant access to a client app on behalf of the user without sharing user credentials.

This method enhances security by using access tokens that are refreshed and transcient, minimizing the risk of unauthorized access.

Secondary user verifiation is a common practice to layer additional security measures for critical tasks, such as requiring explicit assertions (yes/no) or PINs (ex. locking the door would not require the user to say the security PIN, but unlocking the door would).

Competitive Analysis

By evaluating the common feature sets and industry standards of competitors (August Home, U-tec, and Kwikset) regarding the Google Assistant integration, this allowed me to design the user flow.

Design Exploration & Iteration

After scoping and researching the problem space, I spent the next week mapping out potential user entry points for the feature, and designing initial prototypes based around that. I actively sought feedback from the CPO and developers to ensure the designs aligned with the app flip documentation and matched the goal of the feature.

I gathered the feedback through product team roundups (where I shared the designs on a daily basis and delved into various options to explore), alongside developer meetings (to discuss the design constraints vs security protocol).

MVP

Testing & Validation

Using different smart devices on hand (such as the Apple HomePod & Amazon Sidewalk), I followed the OAuth 2.0 authorization flow for Google Assistant with the Level Lock.

This way, I was able to justify my design decisions to the developers regarding what endpoints were necessary for data access between the Level app and the Google Home app.

Results

By having the feature be introduced in onboarding for new users, this would provide a strong inclination to use the feature. Other entry points are within the settings or during set up for another Level device.

Moving Forward

Next Steps

After handoff to the developers, the next step regarding the OAuth designs is enabling a way of switching Google accounts without having to unlink the current account. Since having one account associated to the OAuth is a technical constraint, this is put for later down the roadmap.

Further testing and user feedback is needed to understand the usefulness this feature provides the ~4000 users that use smart home devices.

What I Learned

Level Lock's are currently circulating the United States with +1 million devices sold. This project enables the smart locks to be paired with Google Assistant and paves way for other smart devices to be integrated into the ecosystem.

Throughout this project, it taught me secure user authentication and authorization industry standards and how to design for it based on the technical needs. Understanding permissions and access is important to scaling products and gaining customer trust, and I want to dive deeper into the psychology behind designing for that in a business.
