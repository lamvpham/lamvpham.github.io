Ideation Toolkit

What is the Ideation Toolkit?

The objective of the physical ideation toolkit is to provide students with a portable and cost-effective tool that has an intuitive process and is easy to use. In addition to increasing the quality and quantity of idea generation throughout a project's process, the toolkit will encourage the use of accurate and consistent engineering documentation and brainstorming, with compliance to the RADAR guidelines.



My team created three ideation toolkits: [Insert 'Creativity Here'], Anonyvation, and Connext.



[Insert 'Creativity Here']: A mobile application that sends notifications to users with topics of random ideation questions. The user is then required to take a photo or video of objects around them as inspiration for the sketch sprint that occurs afterwards, based on the question. Once they are done the sprint, it is then uploaded onto the [Insert 'Creativity Here'] app where the user can invite others to comment and rank how they like the design idea.


Anonyvation: A card game in which a die is rolled for a specific themed card prompt and the user has to come up with a solution based on the prompt. Another member of the group then attempts to explain the solution, giving an alternate perspective on the what was conceived.


Connext: A tool for innovators to think outside of the box. Users start by selecting a problem space (based on locations), and then combine provided keywords and icons, as well as their own ideas to create a connected web of concepts.
